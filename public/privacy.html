Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

This site is the personal site of Luke Nealon. This policy explains what data the site and its AI assistant handle, and why. The short version: almost nothing is kept, and nothing personal is stored.

The AI assistant

The site includes a scoped AI assistant that only discusses its own architecture. When you use it:

  • Your messages are sent to Amazon Bedrock (AWS, Sydney region) to generate replies, then discarded. They are not retained after the response.
  • An anonymous, randomly generated session identifier is stored in your browser and used to count messages for rate limiting. It contains no personal information and expires automatically.
  • No message content, names, emails, or identifying data are stored on any server.
  • Your conversations are not used to train any AI model.
  • A small daily usage budget caps the service; beyond it, the assistant pauses until the next day.

You give consent before the assistant activates. You can withdraw it at any time using "Reset privacy" in the assistant, which clears the consent flag, the anonymous session identifier, and the saved conversation from your browser. "New chat" clears the current conversation without affecting your consent.

Browser storage

This site uses your browser's local storage only for:

  • Your theme choice.
  • Your assistant consent flag and anonymous session identifier.

No advertising or third-party tracking cookies are used.

Hosting

The site is served as static files from AWS (S3 and CloudFront). Standard, short-lived access logs may be generated by AWS for security and operations; these are not used to identify or profile visitors.

Your rights

Because no personal data is stored, there is nothing to export or delete on request. Clearing your browser storage removes everything this site has kept about you. For any question about this policy, contact luke@nealon.tech.
